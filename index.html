Hey there!


Gandharv Suri is a final year student at the International Institute of Information Technology, Bangalore pursuing the Integrated Bachelors and Masters in Computer Science program. He can be described as a neophile, as he is always up to work in a new field or with a new tech stack, which is evident by the breadth of knowledge base and experience in different fields he has gained. He is always excited to step outside his comfort zone and try his hand at something new.

He was always excited to work in machine learning, and self-taught prior to opting for the course at IIIT-Bangalore. Going a step further, he opted for a separate semester-long Deep Learning course from Udacity outside the usual curriculum. Wishing to delve into the field of Natural Language Processing, he worked with the Web Science Lab at IIIT-Bangalore, under the supervision of Prof Srinath Srinivasa for a year. He worked on the project ‘Narrative Arc’ which is a AI-based pedagogic agent which makes the learning for users more interactive and personalised. Working with the NLP team for the project, he worked on question generation and summarization tasks for the text corpus and audio transcripts. Taking forward his interest in NLP, he bagged the opportunity of Globalink Mitacs intern and worked under Prof Yllias Chali, at the University of Lethbridge (ULETH). At ULETH he worked on abstractive text summarization and query-based abstractive text summarization, achieving the state-of-the-art zero-shot and few-shot results. As a football fan and enthusiast he, along with a couple of friends, worked under Prof B.Thangaraju on a fantasy football assistant project. They built a web application that suggests the best transfers a user can make, in order to score the maximum number of points in the fantasy game in the following weeks. He explains the application to have turned out better than anticipated, as the team jumped into it with just an idea, and were successful in figuring out the technical aspect of it quickly. Additionally, committed to challenging himself and exploring and enhancing his skill set, he is currently working as an open-source contributor at Modular Open Source Identity Platform (MOSIP). Going into the project, he did not possess the experience with the tech stack that was required by the project but showcasing his agile ability to grasp things quickly, he was able to get started and meet expectations quickly.

Apart from academics, Gandharv also steps up for co-curricular activities. He was a player of the IIIT-Bangalore’s football team and a core member of the IIIT-Bangalore Sports Committee. He was part of the team that organised the first inter-college sports fest ‘Umang’, which went on to be a huge success. He is outgoing and social and gets along well with people. Curious by nature, he is always interested in discussing new ideas with his peers and seniors and learning from other’s experiences. He loves working on innovative ideas, as exemplified by his choice of projects and fields of interest, particularly NLP, where the surface has barely been scratched. Another example is the Fantasy Football Assistant project, where such an application did not exist before this.
